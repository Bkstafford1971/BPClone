Ok, there are some things we need to clean up and fix.

- Training.  Right now if a warrior trains in something, it seems that they are automatically successful.  The success of training should depend on a few things.

 	- The Intelligence of the warrior and the Constitution of the warrior play a big factor.  Higher intelligence makes it easier to learn weapon skills and non weapon skills.
 	- Constitution helps with increasing the attributes.  
		- There should be a formula put on training.  Each skill level that it gained should be harder to attain.  So let's say I wanted to train skills in Initiative.  I have an average intelligence of 12.  I have 0 skills currently in initiative.  With that intelligence and this being the first skill in that area, the success rate should be realistic.  an average intelligence person can pick something up relatively easy, but is not guaranteed.  So for sake of this example, let's say that they initial success rate ios at around 75%.  If the intelligence is increased, the success rate increases.  For example if I increase the Intelligence to 13 that increases my success rate to say 76% or 77%.  Now let's say that my Int is still12 and I successfully learned the first skill level.  expanding on that, so if an intelligence 12 warrior has a 75% chance of learning the first Initiative skill, then the 2nd skill will be slightly more difficult to attain.  Perhaps it drops the success rate say 5-7%.  If the Intelligence is increased, then an increase would raise the success rate 2.5%.   To put it in better terms.  Int 12, 0 skill, a 75% success rate.  Int 12, 1 skill in an area 68% success rate.  Int 12, 2 skills in an area 61%.  Now, if the Int increases from 12 to 13 and the warrior has a level 2 in a skill then the success rate would go from 61% up to 63.5%.  I hope that makes sense.

		- Now with the attribute increases, those are influenced by the Constitution score.  The higher the constitution score, the higher the success rate in increasing attributes.  The percentage success rate is dependent on the Constituition score.  To give a real life example, a warrior with an 18 constitution should be able to increase any one attribute by 5-6 points realtively easy.  If they trained in that attribute 3 times per fight, I would expect them to attain the 6th increase somewhere around their 10th fight.  All increases to that attribute after attaining the 6th increase is much more difficult, regardless of their constitution score.
